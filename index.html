Biological significance (proposed): Your cells are two-thirds water by volume — 99% water molecules by count. Pollack proposes that EZ water is what lines cell membranes, drives blood flow in capillaries, and powers countless biological processes. Fresh organic fruits and vegetables naturally contain EZ water — one reason they're so hydrating and health-promoting. Sunlight energises water inside your body, expanding EZ zones and enhancing cellular function. The absolute pinnacle of hydration is water that has been structured by nature itself.

This is frontier science — not yet part of mainstream consensus, but built on reproducible laboratory experiments that challenge conventional assumptions about the simplest molecule on Earth.

8 Types of Additives in Supplements

Understanding what's inside your supplements beyond the active ingredient:

1. Fillers
The most used additives. Added to bulk up products — the active ingredient is often so small it wouldn't be noticeable without a filler.
2. Binders
Help ingredients stay together. Keep compressed tablets in one piece. Can also serve as fillers in tablets with small active ingredients.
3. Anti-Caking Agents (Flow Agents)
Prevent powder from clumping. Also added to capsule exteriors so they move through machines without sticking. The worst part: they don't need to be labelled in the ingredient list.
4. Disintegrants
Help break down tablets once in your system. Anything labelled "fast-acting" usually contains a disintegrant responsible for this.
5. Coatings & Glazes
Function as glue on the outside of supplements, especially in humid conditions. Enteric coatings dissolve slowly so the supplement bypasses stomach acid and reaches the small intestine or pancreas.
6. Preservatives
Extend shelf life and keep mould, fungi, yeast, and bacteria away. Natural: Vitamins A, C, E, amino acids. Artificial: Benzoates, Parabens, Sulfites, Sorbates, TBHQ (Tertiary Butylhydroquinone), BHA (Butylated Hydroxyanisole).
7. Acidulants
Multi-purpose: enhance flavour, increase shelf life, improve colour/texture, prevent mould, control pH. Common forms: lactic acid, citric acid.
8. Colours & Flavourings
Artificial colours make products appear brighter. Common flavour enhancers are sweeteners — natural (fruit extracts) and artificial (high fructose corn syrup, aspartame, saccharin).
